Structured Role Complexity Framework (SRCF)

SRCF is an independent, transparent point-factor job evaluation framework designed for modern knowledge-based organizations.

The framework evaluates role complexity across three structural dimensions:

Know-How – Required expertise, depth, and human capability

Problem Solving – Cognitive demand, autonomy, and integration complexity

Accountability – Impact scope, decision authority, and organizational consequence

SRCF emphasizes structural contribution and observable role requirements rather than hierarchy or job titles.

Philosophy

Modern knowledge-driven organizations reward:

Scarce expertise

Intellectual depth

Systems integration capability

Strategic impact

Salary bands may overlap across levels.
A highly specialized professional may earn more than an early-stage manager when market demand and structural contribution justify it.

Compensation reflects complexity and impact — not hierarchy alone.

Intellectual Positioning

The SRCF architecture follows the classical three-factor tradition used in established point-factor job evaluation systems.

However, SRCF is independently developed and is not affiliated with, derived from, or endorsed by any proprietary compensation survey provider.

The purpose of this framework is transparency, structural clarity, and modern applicability.
